Caring for Dog Bites: Treatment and Precautions

If you've been bitten by a dog, it's important to act quickly to reduce the risk of infection and assess the severity of the injury. Here's what you should do:

Put Distance Between You and the Dog

  • Immediately move away from the dog to prevent further bites.

Check the Dog’s Vaccination History

  • If the dog is known, ask the owner for its vaccination history, including rabies information. Make sure to get the owner's contact details.
  • If the dog is unaccompanied, ask witnesses for information about the dog and its owner.
  • Ensure your own dog is up-to-date on vaccinations.

Administer First Aid

  • For minor bites (no broken skin):Clean the area with warm water and soap. Apply antibacterial lotion as a precaution.
  • For broken skin:Clean the area with warm water and soap. Apply antibacterial lotion as a precaution.
  • If bleeding:Apply a clean cloth to the wound and gently press to stop the bleeding. Cover with a sterile bandage and apply antibacterial ointment.
  • Monitor for signs of infection:Redness, swelling, warmth, or tenderness. Seek medical attention if the wound worsens or you develop a fever.

Treatment Steps

  • 1. Wash the wound with soap and water.

  • 2 Apply pressure to stop bleeding.

  • 3. Use antibacterial ointment.

  • 4. Cover with a sterile bandage.

  • 5. Monitor for infection.

When to Seek Medical Help

Immediate medical care is needed for dog bites that:

  • Are from a dog with an unknown rabies vaccination history or a sick or erratic dog.
  • Don’t stop bleeding.
  • Cause severe pain.
  • Expose bone, muscle, or tendons.
  • Cause loss of function (e.g., inability to bend fingers).
  • Appear red, swollen, or inflamed.
  • Leak pus or fluid.

Also seek medical help if you:

  • Don’t remember your last tetanus shot.
  • Feel weak, disoriented, or faint.
  • Have a fever.
