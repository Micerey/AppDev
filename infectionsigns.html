Dog Bite Infection Sign

When a dog bite breaks the skin, it can introduce bacteria into the body, potentially causing infections like tetanus, rabies, or sepsis. Immediate and thorough wound care can significantly reduce these risks.

An Infection may cause:

Local Symptoms

  • Swelling and redness around the wound

  • Pain lasting longer than 24 hours

  • Drainage from the wound

  • Difficulty moving the affected area

  • A warm sensation around the wound


Systemic Symptoms:

  • Fever

  • Shaking

  • Night sweats

If you notice signs of infection, such as inflammation, pain, redness, or swelling, seek medical care.

Prevention and Initial Care

To prevent infection:

  • 1. Wash the wound immediately with soap and warm water, cleaning thoroughly.

  • 2. Flush the wound with lukewarm water to remove bacteria.

  • 3. Apply antibiotic cream and cover with a clean bandage.


For deeper wounds:

  • Press a clean, dry cloth against the wound to stop bleeding.

  • Seek medical attention immediately.

  • Call emergency services if bleeding is uncontrollable or if the injured person feels faint.

Specific Infections from Dog Bites

  • Capnocytophaga Infection

    This rare bacterial infection presents symptoms such as:

    1. Blisters, redness, and swelling around the wound

    2. Fever, vomiting, diarrhea, and headaches

    3. Joint pain


    Risk factors include:

    1. A weakened immune system

    2. Excessive alcohol consumption

    3. Lack of a spleen

    Untreated, Capnocytophaga can lead to kidney failure, heart attack, or gangrene. Antibiotics are the primary treatment.

  • Sepsis

    Sepsis is a life-threatening reaction to infection. Symptoms include:

    1. High or low body temperature

    2. Confusion and drowsiness

    3. Severe pain or discomfort

    Sepsis requires immediate medical attention, treated with IV fluids and antibiotics.

  • Rabies

    Rabies symptoms include:

    1. Headache, fever, and weakness

    2. Itching or tingling near the bite site

    Rabies is fatal without treatment. Postexposure vaccination can prevent the disease if administered promptly.

  • Tetanus

    Symptoms of tetanus include:

    1. Jaw cramping and muscle spasms

    2. Difficulty swallowing

    3. Muscle stiffness

    Tetanus is serious and requires immediate treatment with antibiotics and a tetanus vaccine.
